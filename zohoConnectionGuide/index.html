Zoho Remote Connection Guide

Follow this interactive guide to get a client connected via Zoho Assist.

Before We Begin..

Windows or macOS?

Getting Started

To begin, you'll need to navigate to the correct support page. This process should start with making sure they have closed or minimized all open windows! Ask your client if they have a "Data Doctors RemoteMD" icon on their desktop -- it should have a blue "Rx" for an icon! If so, have them open up that shortcut, then continue on.

Example:

"Let's get started by closing or minimizing any open windows that you have up and let's get to your desktop. There you should see a Data Doctors Remote MD icon -- normally it will look like a blue 'R', do you see that? Great, go ahead and double-click on that for me."

Make sure we're at the correct website.

Verify that they are in the right place -- they should be at a Data Doctors (not Google, not Bing, not DuckDuckGo) webpage that says "Connect with us." in the center. If they appear to be at a search page, you can either have them try entering the URL again, and failing that, you may have to navigate them to the correct page manually.

Example:

"Now you should be at a Data Doctors webpage - could you confirm that? It should say 'Remote MD Support Portal' at the top, and if you scroll a little farther you'll see 'Connect with us' in the center of the page."

Getting There Manually

Ask the client to open up their preferred web browser of choice. Once it's loaded, have them click into the address bar at the top of their window and enter the following URL: datadoctors.com/support and press Enter.

Example:

"It looks like we'll have to try to get there another way. Please open up whatever your usual web browser is. Now, at the very top of that window you should have your address bar - go ahead and click in there and I'll give you a website address to type in. Great, now, all spelled out, it will be data doctors dot com."

Entering the Session ID

  • Now that the client is at the correct webpage, we can go to our portal for Zoho Assist and generate a session ID by clicking "Start" next to the pre-scheduled appointment listing.
  • Have the client click in the empty box directly below where it says "Enter Session ID" and then guide them through entering the 9-digit number Zoho generated, then click on the blue "Connect" button.

Example:

"Keep scrolling until you see 'Enter Session ID' - go ahead and click in the blank box directly below that and I'll read you a 9-digit number to enter in there, just let me know when you're ready. Once we're done, go ahead and click on the blue 'Connect' button."

Naming Names

On the next page, there is a box for the client to enter their name. Go ahead and have them enter it, then click on the blue "Join Session" button.

Example:

"On the next page you should see a box for your name - go ahead and put your first name in there and then click on the blue 'Join Session' button."

Download the File

At this point, the client should click on the blue "Agree and Download" button, which will download a file called "ZA_Connect.exe" -- if they are a bit click-happy, or have just had a remote session before, there may be a number trailing the file name, but it should not matter.

This is often the trickiest part of the connection process - be patient.

  1. At first, simply ask the client to open the file.
    • Failing that, have the client, while still in their web browser, press a key combination - ask them to please hold down the Ctrl key and then to tap the "J" key one time - this should open up the downloads view for all Windows-based web browsers. From there, have them open the top-most "ZA_Connect.exe" file.
  2. Let them know that there will be a permissions dialog asking a Yes/No question about whether to let us make changes to their computer - they should click "Yes." If you get this far, click Next Step to continue on.

  3. If the client reports any pop-ups stating that Zoho Assist is already running, just have them click "Okay" or close the message box.

Example:

"Go ahead and click on 'Agree and Download' - now you should see a file downloading called 'ZA_Connect', please run that when it's finished. Okay, if it disappeared on you, let's try something out really quick - I'm going to ask you to press a couple keys on your keyboard, so you might need two hands. Go ahead and hold down the Control key, and then just tap the 'J' key. Great, go ahead and run the ZA_Connect.exe file you see there."

One Last Step!

Your client should now see a Zoho window with a blue "Join" button in the bottom-right corner. Once they click on that, we should be connected!

Example:

"Almost there. If you've already clicked 'Yes' just sit tight - it might look like nothing is happening right now, but in a few moments another window will come up with a blue 'Join' button in the bottom-right corner. Once you click on that, I'll be in."
