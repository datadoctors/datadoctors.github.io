ELLIPSE Element

The ELLIPSE element displays an ellipse on the receipt.

Attributes

None

Value

None

Child Elements

Element

Content

Description

<X1>

Numeric expression

Required. X coordinate of the top left corner of the area in which the ellipse is displayed, in inches from the left edge of the receipt.

<Y1>

Numeric expression

Required. Y coordinate of the top left corner of the area in which the ellipse is displayed, in inches from the top edge of the receipt.

<X2>

Numeric expression

Required. X coordinate of the bottom right corner of the area in which the ellipse is displayed, in inches from the left edge of the receipt.

<Y2>

Numeric expression

Required. Y coordinate of the bottom right corner of the area in which the ellipse is displayed, in inches from the top edge of the receipt.

<PENCOLOR>

Color

Optional. Color of the border of the ellipse.

<PENSTYLE>

  • psSolid

  • psDash

  • psDot

  • psDashDot

  • psDashDotDot

  • psTransparent

  • psInsideSolid

Optional. Pen style of the border of the ellipse. This element is not used if the value of the PENWIDTH element is greater than one.

<PENWIDTH>

Numeric expression

Optional. Width of the border of the ellipse.

<BRUSHCOLOR>

Color

Optional. Fill color of the ellipse.

Remarks

Shapes are drawn in the order in which they appear in the receipt template. Shapes that overlap existing shapes appear on top.

X and Y coordinates are absolute locations on the receipt, and are not affected by other elements in the receipt template.

Example

The following example displays a green ellipse with a blue dash-dot border on the receipt. The ellipse is one inch wide and half a inch tall, and is displayed in the center of the receipt with its top edge 0.5 inches from the top of the receipt.

<SET name="PageWidth" custom="true" description="Width of the page" type="vbString">

    2.75

</SET>

 

<ELLIPSE>

    <X1> (PageWidth / 2) - 0.5 </X1>

    <Y1> 0.5 </Y1>      

    <X2> (PageWidth / 2) + 0.5 </X2>

    <Y2> 1.0 </Y2>

    <PENCOLOR>   vbBlue </PENCOLOR>

    <PENSTYLE>   psDashDot </PENSTYLE>

    <PENWIDTH>   1 </PENWIDTH>

    <BRUSHCOLOR> vbGreen </BRUSHCOLOR>

</ELLIPSE>
